Cycling Health Benefits & Safety

A DC Perspective

Presented by: Luke Aslanian
GPO Safety and Health Awareness Committee
January 14, 2025

The Longevity Effect

  • Cyclists live 2-5 years longer than non-cyclists (British Medical Journal, 2017 study of 250,000 UK commuters)
  • 46% reduced risk of cardiovascular disease and 45% lower risk of cancer (University of Glasgow, 2017)
  • Regular cyclists take 15% fewer sick days (Dutch Economic Institute, 2019)
  • 15-year longitudinal study shows 39% lower mortality rate for bicycle commuters (Danish Heart Foundation, 2020)
    • Sources: BMJ 2017;357:j1456, Lancet Public Health 2022;7:e716-28

Fitness & Physical Health

  • Low-impact exercise perfect for DC's flat terrain
  • Burns 400-750 calories per hour
  • Ideal for commuting to GPO

DC Cycling Community

  • Capital Bikeshare reached 5.7 million rides in 2023
  • DC's protected bike lane network grew by 7.4 miles in 2023, now totaling over 24 miles
  • 40% increase in bicycle commuting since 2019 (DDOT)
  • Over 100 active cycling groups in DC metro area
  • 626 DC organizes 3+ weekly group rides with 50+ regular participants
    • Sources: DDOT 2023 Annual Report, Capital Bikeshare Metrics 2023

Environmental Benefits

  • Each mile cycled instead of driven prevents 0.97 pounds of CO2 emissions (EPA, 2023)
  • DC bike commuters collectively prevent 22,000 metric tons of CO2 annually
  • Cycling infrastructure requires 1/10th maintenance cost compared to roads (DDOT)
  • One car parking space can accommodate 12 bicycles
  • DC's air quality improved 20% in areas with increased cycling (DC Department of Energy & Environment, 2023)
    • Sources: EPA Transportation Emissions Data 2023, DDOT Infrastructure Report 2023, DOEE Air Quality Report 2023

Driver Awareness in DC

  • 75% of bicycle crashes occur at intersections (DDOT Crash Data, 2023)
  • 3-foot passing law in effect since 2016 (DC Code ยง 50-2201.28)
  • 284 reported bicycle crashes in DC in 2023, down 12% from 2022
  • Most dangerous times: 4-7 PM weekdays (DDOT)
  • Top crash factors: failure to yield (38%), improper turning (22%)
  • H Street NE and 14th Street NW identified as high-incident corridors
    • Sources: DDOT Vision Zero Progress Report 2023, MPD Crash Statistics 2023

Cyclist Rights in DC

  • Full lane usage rights on all DC streets
  • Protection under DC's anti-harassment law
  • Right to use left turn lanes
